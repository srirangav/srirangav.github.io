(An amusing anecdote from the Jan 26 San Jose Mercury News.)
 
Nearly everyone knows that Judith Martin, better known as Miss
Manners, the syndicated columnist, is exceedingly correct.  Last
week, she saw an advertisement in the newspaper that a Maryland
jewelry store was having a sale in her silver pattern.  Upon 
arriving at the store, she told the jeweler she was looking for 
additional dessert spoons in her pattern and had been making do 
with the larger soup spoons.

"That's not much of a hardship," the employee said.  "It is for 
me," Martin responded.  Caught up in the moment, the saleswoman 
joked, "Who do you think you are, Miss Manners?"  The easily
recognizable Miss Manners looked at the woman, unable to respond. 
And then it registered.  "Oh my God!" the saleswoman said.
