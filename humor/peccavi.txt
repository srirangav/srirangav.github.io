It is a common idea that the most laconic military dispatch was that
sent by Caesar to the Horse Guards at Rome containing the three
remarkable words 'Veni, vidi, vici' (I came, I saw, I conquered) and
perhaps until our own day, no like instance of brevity has been
found. 

The dispatch of Sir Charles Napier after the capture of Sind to Lord 
Ellenborough, both for brevity and truth, is, however, far beyond it. 
The dispatch consisted of one emphatic word: 

'Peccavi' -- I have Sind (sinned).
